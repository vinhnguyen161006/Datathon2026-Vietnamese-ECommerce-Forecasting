\begin{abstract}
We present a daily revenue and COGS forecasting pipeline for a Vietnamese fashion e-commerce company, covering 01/01/2023--01/07/2024. Our approach trains a LightGBM model on 66 engineered features that include five Facebook Prophet seasonality components (trend, weekly, yearly, seasonal, yhat), evaluated via forward-walk cross-validation (5 folds). Prophet components are used as LGBM input features rather than a fixed blend weight, allowing the tree model to learn adaptively when to trust the seasonality signal. The best Kaggle submission achieved an MAE of \textbf{808,731 VND/day}. We additionally conducted exploratory data analysis across six business domains---customer lifecycle, promotions, returns, inventory, web traffic, and reviews---producing quantified prescriptive recommendations with an identified annual opportunity of over 300M VND.
\end{abstract}

%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Dataset \& Problem Statement}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The dataset covers a Vietnamese fashion e-commerce platform from 04/07/2012 to 31/12/2022 (train) and 01/01/2023 to 01/07/2024 (test). It comprises 14 relational tables including daily sales (target), orders, customers, products, promotions, inventory, web traffic, and reviews (total $\sim$1.3M transaction records across 646,945 orders and 121,930 customers).

\textbf{Task:} Predict daily \texttt{Revenue} and \texttt{COGS} for 548 test days. Evaluation metric is MAE (lower is better). No external data sources are permitted.

\section{Modeling Pipeline}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Cross-Validation Strategy}

We use \textbf{forward-walk cross-validation} (\texttt{TimeSeriesSplit}, $n{=}5$) to respect temporal ordering. KFold shuffle is strictly avoided to prevent leakage. Each fold's training set contains all data up to a cutpoint; validation is the immediately following window.

\subsection{LightGBM}

The primary model is a \texttt{LGBMRegressor} with the following hyperparameters:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \texttt{n\_estimators=5000}, \texttt{learning\_rate=0.01}, \texttt{num\_leaves=31}, \texttt{max\_depth=6}
  \item \texttt{subsample=0.75}, \texttt{colsample\_bytree=0.75}, \texttt{reg\_alpha=0.02}, \texttt{reg\_lambda=0.3}
  \item Early stopping after 100 rounds on a 10\% holdout validation set
\end{itemize}

Training uses the full 2012--2022 dataset (3,833 days after dropping NaN-lag rows). We train two models: one for Revenue, one for COGS.

\subsection{Prophet Components as LGBM Features}

Rather than blending Prophet and LightGBM predictions with a fixed weight, we first fit a standalone Prophet model (yearly and weekly seasonality, multiplicative mode, \texttt{changepoint\_prior\_scale=0.1}, Vietnamese holidays) and extract five in-sample components:

\[
\mathbf{f}^{\text{Prophet}}_t = \bigl[\hat{y}^P_t,\ \text{trend}_t,\ \text{seasonal}_t,\ \text{weekly}_t,\ \text{yearly}_t\bigr]
\]

These five values are concatenated with the other 61 features as LGBM inputs. LightGBM learns \emph{adaptively} when to trust the Prophet signal (e.g., high weight near Tet, low weight during COVID recovery), outperforming a fixed 80/20 blend that treats all days equally. The \texttt{prophet\_weekly} component ranked 5th by SHAP importance, confirming it carries non-redundant weekly signal that \texttt{Revenue\_lag\_7} (which mixes trend with noise) cannot provide.

\subsection{Autoregressive Prediction}

Test predictions are generated \emph{day-by-day}: each day's LightGBM prediction is immediately fed back into the history dict before computing the next day's lag/rolling/EWM features. Prophet components for test dates are pre-computed once (Prophet extrapolates its trend and seasonality forward) and injected into each test row before prediction.

\subsection{COGS Prediction}

COGS is predicted via monthly margin ratios computed from training data:
\[
\widehat{\text{COGS}}_t = \widehat{\text{Revenue}}_t \times \text{margin\_month}(t)
\]
Monthly margins range from 80.1\% (May) to 97.9\% (August), capturing seasonal cost structure without a separate model.

\subsection{Post-COVID Recovery Scaling}

The training period (2012--2022) includes severe COVID-19 disruption (2020--2021). Because the model learns from suppressed revenue years, predictions for 2023--2024 must be scaled upward. We apply a \textbf{smooth linear} multiplicative scale that eliminates the artificial step jump at year boundaries:

\[
\hat{y}^{\text{final}}_t = \hat{y}_t \times s(t), \quad
s(t) = 1.4643 + \frac{t - t_0}{547} \times (1.5057 - 1.4643)
\]

where $t_0 = \text{2023-01-01}$ and the scale reaches 1.5057 on 2024-07-01. This was calibrated to align predicted yearly means with expected post-recovery revenue trajectories.

\section{EDA Highlights \& Business Insights}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Six EDA notebooks cover customer lifecycle, promotions, returns, inventory, web traffic, and reviews. Key prescriptive findings (all quantified from training data):

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Inventory:} 67.3\% of SKU-months experience stockout. Cross-joining inventory with product prices yields an estimated \textbf{annual lost revenue of several hundred million VND}. Safety stock implementation for the top 3 stockout categories (prioritised by lost-revenue×frequency) is the single highest-ROI intervention.
  \item \textbf{Returns:} Wrong-size accounts for the highest return volume. A size recommendation tool reducing wrong-size returns by 20\% saves \textbf{$\sim$10M VND/year} in refund costs with near-zero deployment cost.
  \item \textbf{Customers:} 18.3\% of customers are one-time buyers. A retention campaign converting 10\% of one-timers to repeat buyers would generate substantial incremental LTV, estimated from the gap between one-timer and repeat-buyer average spend.
  \item \textbf{Promotions:} Lines with promotions show higher average revenue vs.\ no-promo baselines. Concentrating volume in the 20--30\% discount band (optimal net revenue) while eliminating low-ROI bands increases net promo revenue.
  \item \textbf{Traffic:} Email campaigns have the lowest bounce rate yet the lowest share of sessions. Rebalancing budget toward email and referral channels (highest session quality) is estimated to yield a 2--3\% revenue uplift.
  \item \textbf{Reviews:} Products rated 4--5 stars are associated with 15--20\% higher average order value. Improving the 12\% of products currently rated below 3.5 (via QC interventions) is the highest-leverage review action.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Our LightGBM model with Prophet components as adaptive input features achieves a Kaggle MAE of \textbf{808,731 VND/day} on the 2023--2024 holdout. The key architectural insight is using Prophet's decomposed seasonality components (weekly, yearly, trend) as LGBM features rather than a fixed ensemble weight, allowing the model to weight the signal differently across seasons, holidays, and growth regimes. EDA across six business domains identifies inventory safety stock, size recommendation, and customer retention as the three highest-ROI interventions, collectively addressing a quantified annual revenue opportunity in the hundreds of millions of VND.
